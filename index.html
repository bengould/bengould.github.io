My Vision

I’m running for Mayor to fight for every voice in Berkeley. As an engineer and a policy analyst, I solve problems and get things done. As a Berkeley native, Downtown resident, and Cal graduate student, I stand for everyone who is trying to make a life in Berkeley.

My life has been dedicated to sustainability - the idea that social justice, environmental protection, and economic prosperity can (and must) be achieved together. I believe together we can accomplish this here in Berkeley.

In the near term, this means that we can address our housing crisis and reduce our carbon footprint without displacing low-income residents.

We can restore and upgrade our parks, historic buildings, and public infrastructure to save water and energy while reducing inequities across the city.

We can continue bringing innovation and small businesses to our Downtown while simultaneously helping our city’s most vulnerable without criminalizing them.

We can restore Berkeley’s leadership in environmental protection and social justice while remaining a place where people of all backgrounds come to make a life.

Berkeley is a unique city that values rich diversity, equal access to opportunity, and leadership in social and environmental issues. It’s easy to see the differences across eight council districts, but I believe these values are what tie us together and make us one Berkeley. As Mayor, I will represent all of Berkeley on the Council, and I’ll fight to make sure it’s a Berkeley that works for you.

Meet Ben

Ben is an engineer and policy analyst currently attending graduate school at UC Berkeley. Born and raised in Berkeley, he graduated from Berkeley High School and UC San Diego, where he studied Biology and Bioengineering.

A driven leader, Ben is the current Chair of the City of Berkeley's Community Environmental Advisory Commission, an elected Delegate on the Graduate Assembly, and a member of the Budget and The Green Initiative Fund Committees, overseeing nearly $1 million in student fees.

Previously, Ben co-founded and led Engineers for a Sustainable World at Berkeley, a group dedicated to implementing sustainability projects in the community. Through this organization, he has helped create and promote a wireless air quality monitoring network, energy and water conservation practices in off-campus housing, and a partnership with the Jefferson Elementary Green Team to build a rainwater harvester at the elementary school.

He has five years of leadership experience and has worked at Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory, The International Council for Clean Transportation, and the Ludwig Institute for Cancer Research. He has also served in a Congressional district office and on the Board of Directors for Engineers for a Sustainable World National.

His most recent work includes advocating for LGBT-inclusive healthcare for students, creating an interactive web visualization of the City of Berkeley's budget, and developing recommendations for City Council to reduce Berkeley's high housing costs.

The themes of Ben’s work are the themes of Berkeley’s greatest challenges: sustainability, equality, and justice. Ben wants to take greater political leadership in the local community to represent your voice on these critical issues.
