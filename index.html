My Vision

I'm running for Mayor to fight for every voice in Berkeley. As an engineer and a policy analyst, my background is in solving problems and getting things done. As a Berkeley native, Downtown resident, and Cal graduate student, I stand for everyone who is trying to make a life in Berkeley.

My life has been dedicated to sustainability: the idea that social justice, environmental protection, and economic prosperity can — and must — be achieved together. I believe together we can accomplish this here in Berkeley.

In the near term, this means that we can address our housing crisis and prevent displacement while reducing our carbon footprint.

We can restore and upgrade our parks, historic buildings, and public infrastructure to save water and energy while increasing their accessibility.

We can continue bringing innovation and small businesses to our Downtown while simultaneously helping our city's most vulnerable, without criminalizing them.

We can restore Berkeley's leadership in environmental protection and social justice while remaining a place where people of all backgrounds come to make a life.

Berkeley is a unique city that values rich diversity, equal access to opportunity, and leadership in social and environmental issues. With such a diverse city, it's easy to see the differences between us, but I believe it's that diversity that makes Berkeley great and brings us together. Across eight Council districts, there's only one Berkeley; and as Mayor, I'll fight to make sure it's a Berkeley that works for you.

Meet Ben

Ben is an engineer, policy analyst, and dual-degree graduate student at UC Berkeley. Born and raised in Berkeley, he graduated from Berkeley High School and UC San Diego, where he studied biology and bioengineering.

A driven leader, Ben is the current Chair of the City of Berkeley's Community Environmental Advisory Commission and a representative on UC Berkeleyâ€™s Graduate Assembly. He currently sits on the Graduate Assembly Budget Committee and The Green Initiative Fund, collectively overseeing nearly $1 million in student fees.

Previously, Ben co-founded and led Engineers for a Sustainable World at Berkeley, a group dedicated to implementing sustainability projects in the community. Through this organization, he helped create and promote a wireless air quality monitoring network on campus, energy and water conservation practices in off-campus housing, and a partnership with students at Jefferson Elementary to build a rainwater harvester on their campus.

Ben has five years of leadership experience and has worked at Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory, The International Council for Clean Transportation, and the Ludwig Institute for Cancer Research. He has also served in a Congressional district office and on the Board of Directors for Engineers for a Sustainable World National.

His most recent work includes advocating for LGBT-inclusive healthcare for students, creating an interactive web visualization of the City of Berkeley's budget, and developing recommendations for City Council to reduce Berkeley's high housing costs.
